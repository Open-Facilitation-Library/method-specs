# Sources — Appreciative Inquiry

Appreciative Inquiry (AI) was developed by **David L. Cooperrider** and **Suresh Srivastva** at Case Western Reserve University. The foundational articulation is Cooperrider & Srivastva, "Appreciative Inquiry in Organizational Life" (*Research in Organizational Change and Development*, Vol. 1, 1987).

This spec is an **independent OFL rendering** of the method, written in our own words and grounded in the widely-published canon of AI practice:

- Cooperrider, D. & Srivastva, S. (1987). *Appreciative Inquiry in Organizational Life.*
- Cooperrider, D., Whitney, D. & Stavros, J. *Appreciative Inquiry Handbook.*
- The 4-D / 5-D cycle (Define, Discovery, Dream, Design, Destiny) as taught across the public AI literature and the AI Commons.

The 5-D cycle and AI's constructionist premise (a group moves toward what it persistently inquires into) are long-established, freely-taught method elements, not the protected expression of any single text. Nothing here is copied or adapted from a copyrighted rendering.
