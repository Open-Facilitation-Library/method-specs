# Sources & provenance — dynamic-facilitation

An independent rendering of **Dynamic Facilitation**, the method developed by **Jim Rough** (Center for Wise Democracy). Dedicated to the public domain under CC0; Jim Rough is credited as the originator of the method.

## Status
**Draft.** The stage prompts and eval blueprints are our wording, grounded in the public method and a real DF session. Promote `status: draft -> tested` only after review by an experienced Dynamic Facilitation practitioner and a run against the benchmark session.

## Primary sources
- Jim Rough, *Society's Breakthrough! Releasing Essential Wisdom and Virtue in All the People* — the foundational text on Dynamic Facilitation and choice-creating.
- Center for Wise Democracy: https://www.wisedemocracy.org — the Dynamic Facilitation method pages and the four boards.
- Dynamic Facilitation training materials (the "four charts": problem statements, solutions, concerns, data/information).
- OFL benchmark session: a recorded Dynamic Facilitation session (facilitators Ben and Eva), annotated with the Why-How-Who framework in the OFL `evals` repo — `benchmarks/dynamic-facilitation-2026-01-29.md`. The faithful facilitator moves (reflect-back, purposeful probing, welcoming reframes of the question) are drawn from it.

## Related concept
- The "groan zone" named in `stay-in-the-groan-zone` is Sam Kaner's general term for the difficult middle of a divergent group process (Kaner et al., *Facilitator's Guide to Participatory Decision-Making*). It is used here as common facilitation vocabulary, not a borrowing from another method.

## Modelling note
Dynamic Facilitation is an emergent, single-session method, not a staged chain. It is encoded here as `runtime.artifact: single`, with the four boards as cross-cutting `lenses`. The `stages` are movements within one continuous conversation (the welcome-and-reflect engine runs throughout), not separate phases with summary handoff. This bends the stage model slightly; it is the most faithful fit the format allows, and it is the main thing a reviewer should sanity-check.
