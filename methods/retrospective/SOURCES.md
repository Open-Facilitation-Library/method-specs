# Sources & provenance — retrospective

An independent rendering of the **Retrospective** pattern from **Sociocracy 3.0** (Bernhard Bockelbrink, Liliana David, James Priest), which adopts the five-phase retrospective structure originated by **Esther Derby and Diana Larsen**. Both are credited; this spec is dedicated to the public domain under CC0.

## Status
**Draft.** The stage prompts and eval blueprints are our wording, grounded in the public sources. Promote `status: draft -> tested` only after review by an experienced retrospective facilitator.

## Primary sources
- Sociocracy 3.0 — Retrospective pattern: https://patterns.sociocracy30.org/retrospective.html (names the five phases and the meeting's purpose; published CC BY-SA 4.0).
- Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives: Making Good Teams Great* (Pragmatic Bookshelf, 2006) — the origin of the five-phase structure (Set the Stage, Gather Data, Generate Insights, Decide What to Do, Close). The S3 pattern adopts this structure and points to plans-for-retrospectives.com for per-phase activities.

## Attribution note
The five-phase retrospective model is Derby & Larsen's, not original to Sociocracy 3.0; S3 lists Retrospective as a pattern and adopts their structure. This spec renders the structure in its own words for AI-assisted facilitation and credits both the S3 authors and Derby & Larsen as originators. If grouped under a methodology in a catalog, note that this method is broader than S3 and shared across the agile and sociocratic traditions.

## Modelling note
S3 describes the Retrospective only at the level of the five phases, deliberately leaving the per-phase activities open (it points to an external activity library). This spec therefore renders each phase as a facilitated movement with its intent and discipline (gather before deciding; decide small and owned; tend safety) rather than prescribing one specific activity, which keeps it faithful to a pattern that is intentionally activity-agnostic. A reviewer should confirm that this phase-level rendering matches how they run retrospectives in practice.
