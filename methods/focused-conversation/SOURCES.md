# Sources — Focused Conversation (ORID)

The Focused Conversation method — widely known by its four levels, **ORID** (Objective, Reflective, Interpretive, Decisional) — was developed by the **Institute of Cultural Affairs (ICA)** as part of the Technology of Participation (ToP). Its best-known practitioner reference is **Brian Stanfield**, *The Art of Focused Conversation: 100 Ways to Access Group Wisdom in the Workplace* (ICA Canada / New Society Publishers, 2000).

This spec is an **independent OFL rendering** of the method, written in our own words and grounded in the widely-taught ORID structure:

- The four-level question sequence (Objective → Reflective → Interpretive → Decisional) as taught across ICA / ToP training and the broad facilitation literature.
- Stanfield, B. (2000). *The Art of Focused Conversation.*

The ORID sequence is a long-established, freely-taught facilitation structure, not the protected expression of any single text. Nothing here is copied or adapted from a copyrighted rendering.
